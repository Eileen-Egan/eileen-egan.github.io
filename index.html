About Me

Hello there! My name is Eileen Egan. I am a senior at the University of Wisconsin Madison studying computer science.

I am involved on campus as the Activities Chair for WACM (ACM's Women in Computing), and as a general member of Girls Who Code. Outside of computer science I am an avid golfer (though admittedly not very good), and I also enjoy snowboarding during the freezing Wisconsin winters. I was raised in Breckenridge, CO and later moved to Lake Geneva WI. Currently I work as a Student Intern Analyst at the Cybersecurity Operations Center here on campus. Last summer I worked at MPX Dev Group as a Cybersecurity Intern, where I got hands-on, real world computer science experience. This summer I am excited to be working at Bank of America through their Global Technology Program! In the past I have worked as a computer science educational assistant, zipline tour guide, and as a snowboard instructor to name a few. Through my experiences in school and the activities I'm involved in I have become extremely adaptable when working on different projects, leading and collaborating with different groups of people, and developing my coding skills.

    Overview

    My primary coding experience comes from the classes I have taken here at UW-Madison. I have become adept in several languages including Java, Python, C, and C++. I have also worked with SQL in my Database Management course! I have worked both on solo and group based projects within my classes. Feel free to read below about some of my favorite projects!

    Comp Sci 400 Projects

    In Comp Sci 400 I worked various roles on a few different projects, like Integration Manager, Backend Developer and Frontend Developer. I was the Integration Manager for our first project in which we created a database to hold various song titles. This project taught me how to integrate different pieces of code together to ensure it still outputted correctly. Furthermore I was the Backend Developer for a project in which we created an app to determine the best route for the starship delivery robots on campus. To do this we set up each location the robot could visit as a node on a Red Black Tree. Then I was in charge of implementing Dijkstra's shortest path algorithm, to calculate the shortest path for the robot.

    Comp Sci 354 Projects

    In Comp Sci 354 I have been able to practice procedural programming, with C. One fun project I did, was I created an Enigma Machine to encode and decode user given inputs. Also I hand coded a linked list in another project. In that project I utilized pointers and structs to maximize the efficiency of my code.
